# About Me

## Career Transition Narrative

My transition into cybersecurity is driven by a commitment to protecting critical systems and a deep interest in how complex technologies operate under real-world conditions.

I am intentionally building toward a career in cybersecurity through a combination of structured education and applied practice. My development focuses on technical depth, disciplined decision-making, and ethical accountability. Formal training through **Merit America** provides a strong foundation, while independent work through **GLITCH (Glitch Cybersecurity Research Lab)** reinforces practical judgment and real-world problem solving.

Cybersecurity sits at the intersection of continuous learning, systems thinking, and public impact — making it a field that aligns closely with my long-term professional goals.

---

## Growth Mindset & Learning Philosophy

I approach cybersecurity as a discipline that requires continuous study and refinement.

My learning process is structured around three core principles:

1. **Foundational Knowledge** — Developing a strong understanding of networking, operating systems, and security fundamentals  
2. **Practical Application** — Applying theory in controlled lab environments through GLITCH  
3. **Reflective Practice** — Documenting findings to reinforce understanding, improve retention, and refine analytical thinking  

Security work requires humility. I approach problems with structured skepticism, evidence-based validation, and a focus on iterative improvement rather than premature conclusions.

---

## Ethical Framework

Integrity is central to my work in cybersecurity.

I operate under the following principles:

- **Authorization First** — I only interact with systems I own or have explicit permission to test  
- **Do No Harm** — The objective is always to strengthen security, not disrupt operations  
- **Privacy & Confidentiality** — Sensitive data is handled with care, respect, and discretion  

All research and testing activities are conducted in authorized and controlled environments.

---

## Professional Trajectory

I am working toward a role in defensive security operations where I can contribute to detection engineering, incident response, and risk analysis.

My long-term focus is on detection architecture and AI-assisted security systems — developing solutions that scale effectively while preserving human oversight, accountability, and sound decision-making.
